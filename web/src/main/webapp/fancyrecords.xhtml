URL Redirection and E-mail Forwarding Records

CustomDNS supports URL and e-mail redirection through so called fancy records. These records are specific to CustomDNS and the underlying infrastructure DNS server Power DNS. These records should not be used if you are having additional secondary/slave DNS with third party providers as they may not replicate correctly.

URL Redirection

URL records allow you to redirect requests to your domain to a different domain. For example, you might wish to redirect all requests to www.example.ca to www.example.com.

To create an URL redirect in the "Create Record" form in the zone in question:

  1. In the "Record Name" field, enter the name of the record to redirect from.
  2. Select "URL" as the Record Type from the "Record Type" selection menu.
  3. In the "Record Content" field, enter the full URL to redirect to.

Figure 1 shows the steps necessary to create a URL redirect

Figure 1: Creating URL redirection records

In Figure 1, any requests to www.nicmus.ca are redirected to http://www.nicmus.com.

There is limited support for wild cards in the URL redirection. One can enter "*.example.ca" to have any request ending on ".example.ca" be redirected.

In order for the URL redirect to function correctly, there must not be an "A" record matching the record name to redirect from.

E-mail Forwarding

E-mail forwarding is provided by the "MBOXFW" record type. E-mail forwarding allows for email addressed to your domain to be forwarded to third party email services. For example, one might wish to have all email addressed to *@example.com be forwarded to yourname@gmail.com.

To create an e-mail forward, in the "Create Record" form for the zone in question:

  1. In the "Record Name" field, enter the email address that mail will be forwarded from.
  2. Select "MBOXFW" from the "Record Type" selection menu.
  3. In the "Record Content" field, enter the full email address that will receive the forwards.
Figure 2: Creating e-mail redirection record

In Figure 2, any email sent to the example.com domain will be redirected to the address specified in the "Record Content field" - in this case yourname@gmail.com

For e-mail forwarding to work, there must not be any MX records in the zone whose email will be forwarded.
